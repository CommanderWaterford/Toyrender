The 6 Most Popular Glasses Frame Styles

1. Aviator Glasses

Originally designed for pilots, aviator frames feature a distinctive teardrop shape with thin metal frames. They work best for people with square or rectangular face shapes, adding softer angles to strong jawlines.

2. Wayfarer Glasses

The timeless wayfarer style features thick plastic frames with a trapezoidal shape. These versatile frames suit almost every face shape and have been a fashion staple since the 1950s. Perfect for both casual and professional settings.

3. Round Glasses

Circular frames add a vintage, intellectual aesthetic. Round glasses work especially well for people with square or rectangular faces, softening angular features. They've become increasingly trendy in recent years.

4. Cat-Eye Glasses

Characterized by upswept outer edges, cat-eye frames add elegance and sophistication. They're particularly flattering for heart-shaped and round faces, creating visual balance and drawing attention to the eyes.

5. Square Glasses

Bold and modern, square frames make a statement. They work well for people with round or oval faces, adding definition and structure. Square frames project confidence and professionalism.

6. Rectangle Glasses

Classic and professional, rectangular frames are the most popular eyewear style worldwide. They suit most face shapes and work in virtually any setting – from boardrooms to coffee shops.

How to Choose Glasses for Your Face Shape

Face shape is the most important factor in choosing flattering glasses. Here's a quick guide:

  • Round Face: Choose angular frames like square or rectangle to add definition
  • Square Face: Opt for round or aviator frames to soften strong angles
  • Oval Face: Lucky you – most styles work well with oval faces
  • Heart-Shaped Face: Cat-eye or round frames balance a wider forehead
  • Rectangle Face: Round or wayfarer frames add width to elongated faces
