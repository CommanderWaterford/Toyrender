Business Photos: What makes them work

Professional photos balance clarity, expression, and context. Keep backgrounds simple, eyes crisp, and posture open. Choose color palettes that reflect your brand.

Quick checklist

  • Lead with a clean headshot (eye contact, soft light, neutral background).
  • Include one context shot (office, boardroom) and one personality shot (speaker or casual).
  • Keep attire tidy; avoid busy patterns; iron collars and lapels.
  • Export sRGB JPEG/WebP at 1200–1600px long edge for platforms like LinkedIn.
  • Write descriptive alt text (lighting, setting, expression, mood) for accessibility & SEO.
  • Add at least one banner‑ratio image (wide crop) for LinkedIn / conference pages.

FAQ: AI business photos

Are AI headshots acceptable on LinkedIn?

Yes, provided they represent your real look faithfully—no drastic changes in features or style.

What’s the ideal first photo?

A bright window-light headshot with a neutral background, slight smile, and shoulders angled.

Do I need a studio?

No—an uncluttered wall and soft window light work great. Toyrender handles the polish and variations.

How many styles should I upload to LinkedIn?

Use one primary headshot and one banner-friendly crop. Keep the rest for your website, CV, or speaker bio.

What’s a good source photo for AI business sets?

Neutral background, sharp eyes, even soft light, minimal shadows, and natural expression. Avoid heavy filters or extreme angles.
